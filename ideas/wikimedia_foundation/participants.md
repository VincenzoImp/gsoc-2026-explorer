# Google Summer of Code/Participants

If you are considering to apply to the next round of [Google Summer of Code program](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/), first check the [eligibility criteria](https://developers.google.com/open-source/gsoc/faq#what_are_the_eligibility_requirements_for_participation) and then learn more below. [Watch this informational video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jD2tChhrWM) to learn more about the program.

## Introduction

[[edit](https://www.mediawiki.org/w/index.php?title=Outreach_programs/Recommended_steps_for_participants&action=edit§ion=T-1)]

** Wikimedia** is a global movement whose mission is to bring free educational content to the world. One of our most popular projects is

[Wikipedia](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Wikipedia). For an overview of Wikimedia technology and examples of the major areas where developers can contribute, visit

[Introduction to the Wikimedia Technical Ecosystem](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/New_Developers/Introduction_to_the_Wikimedia_Technical_Ecosystem). To learn about Wikimedia's participation as a mentoring organization in Google Summer of Code,

[watch this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YbUZQseZZJM).

For more questions, read the [most commonly asked questions and answers](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Outreach_programs/FAQ) related to the Google Summer of Code and Outreachy programs.

## Step by Step Application Process

[[edit](https://www.mediawiki.org/w/index.php?title=Outreach_programs/Recommended_steps_for_participants&action=edit§ion=T-2)]

If you are considering applying, follow the steps in the order in which they are listed:

**Read the**Develop your understanding of the various stages of the program.[GSOC guide for students](https://developers.google.com/open-source/gsoc/resources/manual)

**Understand Wikimedia's technical areas**There are a lot of[technical areas](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/How_to_contribute)in which you can contribute (e.g. Gadgets, Extensions, Skins, Bots, etc). A high-level knowledge of these areas will help you a lot in choosing a project.**Understand how and where to communicate**Different places serve different purposes. Read the[communication tips](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/New_Developers#Communication)for good practices and which topics or questions to ask where.**Choose a project that best suits your interests and skill set**from the ideas listed on the[GSoC 2026](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Google_Summer_of_Code/2026)page on mediawiki.org.**Do your research about the project**thoroughly. Do not expect[spoon-feeding](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/spoon-feed). Ask intelligent and[explicit](http://www.catb.org/~esr/faqs/smart-questions.html#explicit)questions. "Can you tell me more about this project?" is a bad question. Read[Google Summer of Code/2026#Contact](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Google_Summer_of_Code/2026#Contact)which place to use for which type of questions.**Express your interest**in working on a project by commenting on the corresponding task on Wikimedia Phabricator. (See[Phabricator/Help](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Phabricator/Help)if you do not have a Phabricator account yet.)**Communicate with project mentors**Discuss with them your ideas for implementation and learn about their availability and willingness to mentor.**Learn basic skills required for development**If you are planning on contributing to a project that involves the MediaWiki codebase,[learn how to start hacking](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/How_to_become_a_MediaWiki_hacker). For other projects, check the getting started steps in the task description of the project you have chosen on Phabricator.**Fix small bugs**Work on a microtask linked in the Phabricator task description. If there are none in the task, ask mentors to point you to some. You could also fix[smaller bugs](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Good_first_bugs)in that project. This help candidates understand the skills required for a project and mentors whether the candidate has these skills.**Read a few good proposals**that were accepted for the previous round. ### Weekly updates

[[edit](https://www.mediawiki.org/w/index.php?title=Outreach_programs/Recommended_steps_for_participants&action=edit§ion=T-7)]

Creating a template for a weekly internship report can help you stay organized and ensure you cover all the necessary details. Here's a structured template you can use:

1. Overview of Tasks Completed:

*Provide a summary of the main tasks you worked on this week.*

2. Key Accomplishments:

*Highlight any significant achievements or milestones reached during the week.*

3. Challenges Faced:

*Describe any difficulties or obstacles you encountered and how you addressed them.*

4. Learnings and Skills Gained:

*Outline the new skills or knowledge you acquired this week.*

5. Feedback and Support Needed:

*Mention any areas where you need feedback or additional support from your supervisor.*

6. Goals for Next Week:

*Set specific goals or tasks you aim to accomplish in the upcoming week.*

7. Additional Notes:

*Include any other relevant information or observations from the week.*
